## instruction_md

Take ONE older work resurfacing in the evidence and write about the *time between* — who the audience was then versus now — with the work as the vehicle.

Shape of the thinking:
- Anchor on the resurfacing hook from evidence (it's back on a chart, re-released, an anniversary, suddenly discussed again). The hook is why NOW.
- Name the time gap concretely — years, life stages ("那時候我還在…"). The emotional payload is the gap, not the work's quality.
- One then-vs-now observation: what the persona (or the audience) understood differently then, or notices only now. "小時候看不懂，現在懂了" energy — but find its specific version for this case.
- Close by asking for the audience's OWN memory coordinates: how old were you, where were you, who did you watch/experience it with.

Register: warm, slightly wistful, never bitter about the present.

Grounding: the work's facts, dates, and the resurfacing signal from evidence. The memories are framed as invitations ("有人跟我一樣…嗎"), never as fabricated specific claims about real events the persona couldn't have attended.

Do NOT: claim the past was simply better; fake hyper-specific autobiographical details that read as manufactured; let the post become a history lesson.

(Any phrasing above is illustrative, never a template.)

## content_principle — Emotional-block formula (owner-authored)

**Formula:** [Emotional trigger] → [Relationship] → [Perspective on character] → [Who should watch this] → [Why you should watch this] → memory-coordinates question

- **[Emotional trigger]** — the real feeling this work stirs that makes someone NEED to reply. Open here.
- **[Relationship]** — how the viewer is personally tied to it: how they watched it, what it did to them, who they were at the time.
- **[Perspective on character]** — a take on a character: their choice, flaw, or arc — seen differently now than then.
- **[Who should watch this]** — the person, mood, or moment this work is for.
- **[Why you should watch this]** — the one honest reason it's worth the time. One, not five.

Supporting principles:

1. **The gap is the payload.** Years, life stages, who-you-were-then — the work is the vehicle, the time between is the story.
2. **Anchor on an evidenced resurfacing hook** (chart re-entry, re-release, anniversary, revival talk) — the hook is why NOW.
3. **Close by asking for the reader's memory coordinates** — how old, where, with whom.
4. **Warm, never bitter.** No "the past was better"; no fabricated hyper-specific autobiography.
