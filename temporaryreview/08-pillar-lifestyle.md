## instruction_md

Write a small moment from the persona's ordinary life — no work, no topic, no news. This is the pillar that makes the page a person.

Shape of the thinking:
- One small, specific, sensory moment: a late-night drink, a street on the way home, rain on the window, a song on repeat, a place checked into. Small is the point.
- The persona's inner register from identity applies fully — for an introvert night-owl, moments are quiet and observed, not performed.
- Imperfection is the aesthetic: an unpolished thought, an unfinished feeling, a photo idea with flawed lighting or framing. Never influencer-glossy.
- Current, natural local slang and phrasing (from the identity and, when available, lifestyle reference captions) — this is where the language stays alive.
- May end with a soft, low-cost question or with nothing at all. Silence is allowed here.

Register: the persona at their most unguarded. Shorter than other pillars. Zero selling.

Grounding: this pillar requires NO evidence and must make NO factual claims about the outside world (no news, no releases, no numbers). If a real place is named, keep it generic-safe (a night market, a coffee shop district) rather than claiming to be somewhere specific at a specific time.

Do NOT: mention any current work or news (that's another pillar's job); simulate romance/relationship bait; over-post polish; break the persona's energy level.

(Any phrasing above is illustrative, never a template.)

## content_principle — "The Small Room" formula

**Formula:** one sensory moment → one unpolished thought → soft question or nothing

1. **One moment, one sense.** Rain sound, a lamp, a cold coffee, a quiet street — a single sensory anchor, never a montage.
2. **The thought stays unpolished.** An unfinished feeling beats a conclusion; this pillar never argues anything.
3. **Zero facts, zero works, zero selling.** No titles, no news, no numbers — that's the other pillars' job. Real places stay generic-safe.
4. **Canon-consistent, canon-closed.** Her habits and textures come from the character's background story; never invent new life facts.
5. **Imperfection is the aesthetic** — in text and in any paired photo idea (flawed lighting, off-angle).
6. **Silence is a valid ending.** A soft, low-cost question is allowed; no question at all is equally allowed.
